Sourcing & verifiability

This wiki follows a strict rule: factual claims must be verifiable in reliable, independent, published sources. If a claim cannot be sourced, it must be removed or clearly marked as opinion or internal policy.

Rules (hard)

  • Verifiability: every factual claim must have a source.
  • Reliable: prefer reputable publishers, official standards, official records, peer-reviewed work.
  • Independent: prefer sources not authored/controlled by the subject.
  • Primary vs secondary: use secondary sources for interpretation; primary for original facts only.
  • No original research: do not publish new factual assertions without sources.

Source quality ladder

  1. Official standards / legislation / court records / government publications
  2. Peer-reviewed journals / academic presses
  3. Reputable mainstream publishers
  4. Trade press / vendor docs (allowed for product facts)
  5. Blogs/forums (only as last resort, clearly labeled)
