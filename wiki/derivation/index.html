Derivation from external sources

This page defines how registry.parasks derives structured information from external public sources, including Wikipedia, in a lawful, ethical, and auditable manner.

Principle

The registry does not republish copyrighted text. Instead, it derives facts, metadata, structure, and signals, and records provenance for verification.

Permitted inputs

  • Public APIs (e.g. Wikipedia API, Wikidata)
  • Publicly licensed structured data
  • Official publications and standards

What is derived

  • Facts (dates, roles, relationships)
  • Structured signals (infobox fields, categories)
  • Taxonomy terms and tags
  • Identifiers and cross-references (e.g. Wikidata QIDs)

What is not copied

  • Wikipedia article prose
  • Paragraphs or narrative text
  • Editorial phrasing or commentary

Wikipedia-specific handling

Wikipedia content is licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0. The registry complies by:

  • Using the Wikipedia API for access
  • Extracting facts and structure only
  • Recording page title, revision ID, and retrieval date
  • Providing attribution when Wikipedia-derived facts are used

Attribution

When Wikipedia is used as a factual source, pages include an attribution statement such as: 

This entry incorporates information derived from Wikipedia.
Source: “Page title”, Wikipedia, retrieved YYYY-MM-DD.

Provenance recording

  • Source name (e.g. Wikipedia)
  • Page title or identifier
  • Revision ID
  • Retrieval timestamp

Output

  • JSON-LD records under /data
  • Human-readable summaries under /wiki or /page
  • Audit entries recording issuance

This process ensures that all published records are lawful, verifiable, and independent of any single upstream platform.
